Hi! I'm glad you made it. Here's a little about me:


I spent 2 years in the U.S. Navy as an IT on a submarine. I was unfortunately discharged(Honorably) in June of 2021 due to
medical complications that wouldn't allow me to be on a submarine anymore, according to the Navy's standards.

I trained in communications and then merged with IT so I got to train in IT as well.
I strongly value the time I spent in the Navy. Everything has to be perfect in the military therefore you're expected to be perfect.
While we know not everyone is perfect, the life skills and lessons I gained got me a whole lot closer.

These skills have stayed with me since transitioning out of the military and help me in my every day life. The biggest being how to handle
stressful situations. Being on a submarine was one of the most stressful things I could have ever imagined and learning to stay composed,
calm, and collected while getting my work done was paramount for success.


Why I chose software engineering


The Navy was really my first experience being in the tech world and I loved it. The rigorous classroom technical training
was fascinating to me. I even got to code some while training! Once I got out of the Navy I knew I wanted to stay in the tech industry
and wanted to get in it as fast as I could.

That being said, I decided to enroll in the Full Stack Web Development Bootcamp through the University of Texas at Austin.
What a ride that was! It was intense and fast-paced and I got to learn way more than I could have imagined I would.

If you made it this far, my goal is to bring you, (hopefully) my future employer, the best version of myself.
All of my experience and skills gained from the U.S. Navy, and all of my recently gained software developer skills, could all be yours.
